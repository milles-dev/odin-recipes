Pesto Lasagna

Lasagne al Pesto - a nutty twist on an Italian classic

It's all about the layers in lasagne (yes, spelled with an "e" â€” in Italian, lasagna refers to a single sheet of the wide, flat pasta), the beloved baked dish that hails from Emilia-Romagna. We love this recipe for pesto lasagne, which combines simple fresh ingredients to create a complex symphony of flavors

For the Pesto Sacue
  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  • 1 clove garlic, peeled
  • 1 pinch sea salt
  • 1/4 cup freshly-grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • 3 tablespoons freshly-grated Pecorino Romano
  • 5 ounces Ligurian extra virgin olive oil, plust more to to store
For the Lasanga
  • 1 recipe egg pasta dough rolled to thinnest setting on pasta machine, or dry lasagne
  • 1 recipe besciamella (beschamel sauce)
  • 1 cup heritots verts or green beans
  • 1 cup graded Pecorino Sardo or Pecorino Romano
